Open Source Nexus Modelling Tools for Planning Sustainable Energy Transition in Africa

ONEPlanET - Comprehensive Technical Documentation

Duration: 2020 - 2024 Funding: EU Horizon 2020 - â‚¬4.2M Role: Technical Lead & Platform Architect

Executive Summary

ONEPlanET (Open Source Nexus Modelling Tools for Planning Sustainable Energy Transition in Africa) represents a groundbreaking initiative in developing open-source, integrated energy systems modelling tools specifically designed for African contexts. This comprehensive platform addresses the critical need for evidence-based energy planning while enabling sustainable development pathways across the continent.

Key Project Outcomes

  • Developed comprehensive open-source energy modelling platform integrating 15 African countries
  • Created CLEWS (Climate-Land-Energy-Water-Systems) framework for nexus analysis
  • Trained 50+ energy planners across Africa in OSeMOSYS and CLEWS methodologies
  • Generated renewable energy transition scenarios for 85% renewable targets by 2050
  • Established sustainable developer community ensuring platform continuity and innovation

Project Background & Context

Energy Challenge

Africa faces enormous energy access challenges with over 600 million people lacking electricity access. Simultaneously, the continent has vast renewable energy potential that remains largely untapped due to inadequate planning tools and capacity.

Planning Gaps

  • Limited access to sophisticated energy modelling tools
  • Lack of integrated nexus approach in energy planning
  • Insufficient technical capacity for scenario analysis
  • Proprietary software costs limiting accessibility
  • Limited collaboration between African energy planners

Project Objectives

  • Develop open-source energy systems modelling tools for Africa
  • Create integrated Climate-Land-Energy-Water nexus analysis framework
  • Build technical capacity among African energy planners
  • Generate evidence-based renewable energy transition scenarios
  • Foster regional collaboration and knowledge sharing

Technical Methodology & Platform Architecture

Phase 1: Open Source Platform Development

Development of integrated modelling platform based on OSeMOSYS (Open Source Energy Modelling System) with African-specific adaptations and enhancements.

OSeMOSYS Enhancement

  • Enhanced linear programming optimization algorithms
  • African energy technology database integration
  • Multi-scenario analysis capabilities
  • Renewable energy resource assessment tools

Data Infrastructure

  • Continental energy resource databases
  • Technology cost and performance datasets
  • Demand projection methodologies
  • Climate impact assessment tools

Phase 2: CLEWS Framework Implementation

Development of integrated Climate-Land-Energy-Water-Systems (CLEWS) framework enabling comprehensive nexus analysis for sustainable development planning.

Climate Module

Integration of climate projections and extreme weather impacts on energy infrastructure and demand patterns.

Water-Energy Nexus

Modeling of water requirements for energy production and energy needs for water services across different scenarios.

Land Use Integration

Assessment of land requirements for renewable energy deployment and bioenergy production in relation to food security.

Phase 3: Capacity Building & Implementation

Comprehensive training and capacity building program for African energy planners and policymakers.

Technical Training

Intensive training workshops on OSeMOSYS and CLEWS methodologies for 50+ energy professionals across 15 countries.

Country Applications

Development of country-specific energy models and renewable energy transition scenarios with national teams.

Regional Networks

Establishment of African energy modelling network facilitating ongoing collaboration and knowledge exchange.

Challenges & Lessons Learned

Technical Challenges

Data Availability

Limited availability of high-quality energy data across African countries required development of robust data estimation and validation methodologies.

Platform Scalability

Ensuring platform performance with large-scale country models required optimization of algorithms and computational efficiency improvements.

Technology Transfer

Balancing technical sophistication with user-friendliness required iterative design and extensive user feedback incorporation.

Success Factors

Open Source Approach

Open source licensing enabled wide adoption, collaborative development, and transparency, fostering trust and sustainability within the African energy community.

Capacity Building Focus

Extensive training and support programs created lasting technical capacity and ensured successful platform adoption beyond project completion.

Regional Collaboration

Pan-African approach facilitated knowledge sharing, best practice exchange, and development of regional energy integration strategies.

Key Recommendations

  1. Sustained Capacity Building: Continue investment in technical training and institutional capacity development
  2. Data Infrastructure: Strengthen energy data collection and sharing mechanisms across African countries
  3. Regional Integration: Develop regional energy models to support cross-border renewable energy projects
  4. Policy Integration: Integrate modelling results into national energy policy development processes
  5. Community Sustainability: Support ongoing developer community growth and platform evolution
