Water-Energy-Food Nexus Ecosystem-Based Adaptation

Comprehensive Project Report

Duration: 2022 - 2025 Funding: EU Horizon Europe - €2.1M Role: Lead Scientist & Project Coordinator

Executive Summary

The Water-Energy-Food (WEF) Nexus Ecosystem-Based Adaptation project represents a groundbreaking approach to integrated resource management in semi-arid Africa. Implemented in Kenya's Ewaso Ng'iro North Catchment, this EU Horizon Europe-funded initiative demonstrates how traditional ecological knowledge can be successfully integrated with modern scientific approaches to build climate resilience and sustainable livelihoods.

Key Project Outcomes

  • Developed the first comprehensive WEF nexus model for semi-arid Kenya, integrating water, energy, and food security considerations
  • Restored 500+ hectares of degraded ecosystem through community-led initiatives
  • Improved water security for 25,000+ people across 15 communities
  • Generated policy recommendations adopted by Kenya's national water sector strategy
  • Created a replicable framework for ecosystem-based adaptation across East Africa

Project Background & Context

Study Area

The Ewaso Ng'iro North Catchment covers 15,000 km² in northern Kenya, characterized by semi-arid climate, diverse ecosystems, and complex water-energy-food interdependencies. The region supports over 1.2 million people, including pastoralist and agro-pastoralist communities highly vulnerable to climate variability.

Key Challenges

  • Increasing frequency and intensity of droughts
  • Degradation of critical watersheds and rangelands
  • Competition for limited water resources
  • Energy poverty affecting water access and food security
  • Limited integration between sectoral planning approaches

Project Objectives

  • Develop integrated WEF nexus framework for ecosystem-based adaptation
  • Enhance community resilience to climate change impacts
  • Restore degraded ecosystems through participatory approaches
  • Generate evidence-based policy recommendations
  • Build local capacity for sustainable resource management

Methodology & Approach

Phase 1: Baseline Assessment & Stakeholder Mapping

Comprehensive assessment of current WEF nexus dynamics, ecosystem conditions, and stakeholder networks using mixed-methods approach.

Technical Assessment

  • Hydrological modeling using WEAP software
  • Remote sensing analysis for land cover change detection
  • Energy demand and supply mapping
  • Food security and nutrition surveys

Social Assessment

  • Participatory rural appraisal with 150+ community members
  • Traditional ecological knowledge documentation
  • Institutional mapping and governance analysis
  • Gender and social inclusion assessment

Phase 2: Integrated Modeling & Scenario Development

Development of coupled WEF nexus model integrating biophysical and socio-economic components to assess adaptation scenarios.

Water Component

WEAP-based water allocation model incorporating climate variability, demand projections, and ecosystem water requirements.

Energy Component

Energy demand modeling for water pumping, irrigation, and household needs with renewable energy potential assessment.

Food Component

Agricultural production model linking water availability, energy access, and food security outcomes.

Phase 3: Community-Based Implementation

Co-design and implementation of ecosystem-based adaptation interventions with local communities.

Watershed Restoration

Community-led restoration of 500+ hectares using indigenous tree species and traditional water harvesting techniques.

Water Infrastructure

Solar-powered water systems and improved storage facilities serving 12 community groups.

Livelihood Diversification

Climate-smart agriculture, beekeeping, and eco-tourism initiatives to reduce vulnerability.

Key Findings & Results

Quantitative Results

30% Increase in watershed forest cover through community restoration
25% Improvement in water quality indices across monitoring sites
40% Reduction in soil erosion rates in restored areas
60% Reduction in energy costs through solar-powered systems

Social Impact

Community Empowerment

Established 12 community-based natural resource management groups with enhanced technical and leadership capacity.

Gender Integration

Women's participation in water management increased from 25% to 65% through targeted capacity building programs.

Knowledge Integration

Successfully documented and integrated traditional ecological practices with modern ecosystem restoration techniques.

Environmental Outcomes

Biodiversity Recovery

35% increase in indigenous plant species density and 40% improvement in wildlife habitat quality indices in restored areas.

Carbon Sequestration

Estimated 2,500 tons CO2 equivalent sequestered through restoration activities over the project period.

Watershed Health

Improved hydrological function with increased baseflow and reduced peak flows during storm events.

Lessons Learned & Recommendations

Successful Approaches

Participatory Co-Design

Early and continuous engagement of communities in problem definition and solution design proved critical for project success and sustainability.

Traditional Knowledge Integration

Combining indigenous ecological practices with modern techniques resulted in more effective and culturally appropriate solutions.

Multi-Scale Approach

Addressing WEF nexus challenges required interventions at multiple scales from household to catchment level.

Key Challenges

Institutional Coordination

Coordinating across multiple government agencies and sectors required significant time investment and relationship building.

Climate Variability

Extreme weather events during implementation required adaptive management and flexible intervention strategies.

Long-term Financing

Securing sustainable financing mechanisms for continued ecosystem management beyond project period remains challenging.

Policy Recommendations

  1. Integrated Planning: Develop integrated WEF nexus planning frameworks at county and national levels
  2. Community Tenure: Strengthen community land and resource tenure to incentivize long-term ecosystem stewardship
  3. Financing Mechanisms: Establish payment for ecosystem services schemes to sustain restoration efforts
  4. Capacity Building: Invest in technical and institutional capacity for integrated resource management
  5. Knowledge Systems: Create platforms for documenting and sharing traditional ecological knowledge
