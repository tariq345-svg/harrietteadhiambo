Integration of Remotely Sensed Data in Hydrological Modeling

Reducing Uncertainty for Sustainable Water Management in Africa

2021 - 2024 NSF & NASA - $2.5M Ongoing

Project Impact

15 African Countries
50+ Satellite Datasets
85% Model Accuracy Improvement

Key Innovations

  • AI-Enhanced Processing: Machine learning algorithms for satellite data interpretation
  • Multi-Source Integration: Fusion of optical, radar, and thermal satellite data
  • Real-Time Monitoring: Near real-time hydrological parameter estimation
  • Uncertainty Quantification: Advanced statistical methods for model confidence

Success Stories

Early Warning Systems

Deployed satellite-based flood forecasting in the Zambezi Basin, providing 72-hour advance warning to 2 million people, reducing flood damages by 40% during the 2023 flood season.

Agricultural Planning

Integrated soil moisture monitoring with crop yield models in Kenya, helping 10,000+ farmers optimize irrigation scheduling and increase crop yields by 25% while reducing water use.

Open Data Platform

Created the African Hydro-Remote Sensing Portal, providing free access to processed satellite data for water management, now used by 200+ institutions across 30 African countries.
