Towards Achieving Land Degradation Neutrality

Enhanced Food Security and Climate Resilience in Tharaka-Nithi County

2018 - 2021 UNEP & Green Belt Movement - $900K Completed

Project Impact

5,000 Hectares Restored
15,000 Households Benefited
40% Crop Yield Increase

Key Innovations

  • Integrated Landscape Approach: Holistic restoration combining agriculture, forestry, and water management
  • Community-Led Implementation: Farmer-managed restoration with technical support
  • Climate-Smart Agriculture: Drought-resistant crops and sustainable farming practices
  • Digital Monitoring: Satellite-based tracking of restoration progress

Restoration Achievements

2.5M Trees planted across degraded landscapes
75% Reduction in soil erosion rates
300+ Farmer groups established
85% Increase in water table levels

Restoration Technologies

Agroforestry Systems

Integration of trees with crops for improved soil health

Terracing & Contours

Soil and water conservation structures

Water Harvesting

Rainwater collection and storage systems

Cover Cropping

Soil protection and fertility improvement

Community Engagement

Local Organizations

  • 300 Community-Based Organizations
  • 150 Women's Groups
  • 80 Youth Groups

Training Programs

  • 5,000 farmers trained in sustainable practices
  • 200 extension officers capacitated
  • 50 master trainers developed

Environmental Benefits

50,000 Tons CO2 sequestered annually
60% Improvement in vegetation cover
80% Increase in biodiversity indices
45% Reduction in surface runoff

Success Stories

Food Security Transformation

Mary Wanjiku's farm increased maize yields from 2 to 8 bags per acre through integrated soil and water conservation practices, enabling her to feed her family and sell surplus produce.

Water Resource Recovery

The Kathita River sub-catchment saw a 30% increase in dry season flow after 3 years of watershed restoration, benefiting 5,000+ people with improved water access.

Economic Empowerment

Women's groups in Marimanti increased their income by 250% through sustainable beekeeping and indigenous tree nurseries, creating new livelihood opportunities.
